SOURCES & METHODOLOGY

OUR STANCE. We aim for transparency, not the illusion of neutrality. "Government overreach" is itself a framing, and every outlet and think-tank carries perspective. So rather than claim to be unbiased, we show you exactly where each number and headline comes from, date it, and link out. Judge the sources yourself.

Spending data — official government feeds

The numbers on the dashboard come from free, authoritative U.S. government APIs. They are machine-fetched on a schedule and stamped with a fetch date. We do not hand-edit figures.

SourceWhat we pullKey?Lean
Treasury Fiscal DataOutlays, deficit, national debtNoneOfficial / non-partisan
USAspending.govFederal awards & agency spendingNoneOfficial / non-partisan
FRED (St. Louis Fed)Debt, deficit & spending as % of GDPFree keyOfficial / non-partisan

The %-of-GDP ratios are pulled from these specific FRED series: GFDEGDQ188S (debt as % of GDP), FYFSGDA188S (deficit as % of GDP), FYONGDA188S (net outlays as % of GDP).

Context & analysis — think tanks

Interpretation ("what does this number mean?") is drawn from research institutions and clearly separated from the raw data. These organizations do analysis, not neutral reporting, and generally sit center to center-left. We cite them per metric.

News — headlines only, curated by approval

The news feed shows a headline, a short summary, and a link out — never a full article. A scheduled job surfaces candidate stories from the feeds below; a human approves each before it publishes. Lean labels are our best-effort assessment shown for transparency.

How to read this site

  • DATA badge = machine-fetched from a government API.
  • COMMENTARY badge = analysis/opinion, not straight reporting.
  • Lean colors: left center right — our assessment, not an endorsement.
  • Every figure carries a fetched date; check it for freshness.
