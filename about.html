ABOUT

OVERSIGHT TERMINAL is a public, open-source terminal for understanding how the U.S. federal government spends money, and for keeping an eye on questions of government overreach. It is built for the ordinary reader: big numbers, plain language, and a link to the source behind every claim.

Principles

  • Data first. The dashboard leads. Numbers come from official government feeds and are dated.
  • Show your work. Every figure and headline links to its origin. Analysis is labeled and kept apart from data.
  • Transparency over false neutrality. We label source lean openly instead of pretending it doesn't exist.
  • Link, don't reproduce. News items are headlines and short summaries that send you to the original reporting.

How it's built

A static site — no tracking, no accounts, no server. Government spending figures are refreshed automatically by a scheduled job that reads official APIs. Candidate news stories are surfaced automatically but only publish after a human approves them. The retro-terminal styling is an original design and is not affiliated with any game, studio, or agency.

NOT ADVICE. Nothing here is financial, investment, legal, or voting advice. It is a transparency tool. Verify anything important against the linked primary sources.

Accessibility

Prefer a calmer screen? Use [ EFFECTS: OFF ] in the top bar to disable scanlines and flicker and raise contrast. The site also honors your system's "reduce motion" setting automatically.
